Speaking Section: Master the AI's Expectations

The Speaking section is where most students struggle, not because of poor English, but because they don't understand what the AI is listening for.

Read Aloud (6-7 questions)

This is your chance to score easy points, but also where many students lose marks due to poor pronunciation and timing.

‚úÖ Proven Read Aloud Strategy

  1. Preparation time (30-40 seconds): Scan for difficult words, proper nouns, and numbers
  2. Speaking: Speak at normal pace, don't rush
  3. Tone: Keep it monotone - the AI doesn't like dramatic variations
  4. Pauses: Natural pauses at commas and periods only
  5. Mistakes: If you mispronounce, keep going - don't repeat

Repeat Sentence (10-12 questions)

This task tests your listening AND speaking. The key is understanding that the AI values accuracy over perfection.

üí° Repeat Sentence Hack

You don't need to remember every word perfectly. The AI gives partial credit. Focus on getting the main keywords and sentence structure right. Missing small words like "the" or "a" won't kill your score.

Describe Image (6-7 questions)

Many students overcomplicate this. The AI wants fluency and content, not creativity.

Perfect Describe Image Template

Opening (5 seconds): "This image shows..." or "This chart displays..."

Main content (30 seconds): Describe 3-4 key elements with specific details

Closing (5 seconds): "In conclusion, this image effectively shows..."

Writing Section: Where Spelling Can Make or Break You

The Writing section has two tasks, but here's what's crucial: spelling mistakes can destroy your score faster than poor grammar. This is where many students lose their dream scores.

Summarize Written Text (2-3 tasks)

You get 200-300 words to read and must write a summary in one sentence of 5-75 words.

‚úÖ SWT Winning Formula

  1. Read actively: Identify the main idea and 2-3 supporting points
  2. Use connectors: "The passage discusses... because... and... therefore..."
  3. Stay within limits: Aim for 35-50 words for maximum safety
  4. Check spelling: Even one spelling mistake can cost 2-3 points

Essay Writing (1-2 tasks)

The 20-minute essay is where strong preparation pays off. You need a system, not just good English.

‚ö†Ô∏è Essay Killer Mistakes

  • Spelling errors in common words (accommodate, necessary, government)
  • Going under 200 words or over 300 words
  • No clear introduction/conclusion structure
  • Repeating the same vocabulary

Reading Section: Speed and Strategy

Reading is often considered the "easiest" section, but that's a dangerous assumption. The time pressure is real, and the AI penalizes wrong answers more than you think.

Multiple Choice Questions

Both single and multiple answer questions require different strategies.

üí° MCQ Strategy

  • Single answer: If unsure, guess. No negative marking.
  • Multiple answer: Only choose if you're confident. Wrong answers hurt your score.
  • Time management: Don't spend more than 2 minutes on any single question.

Fill in the Blanks

This task appears in both Reading and Reading & Writing sections. Grammar knowledge is key.

‚úÖ Fill in the Blanks Technique

  1. Read the entire passage first for context
  2. Look for grammatical clues (verb tenses, prepositions, articles)
  3. Use collocation knowledge (words that naturally go together)
  4. Read your final answer to check it makes sense

Listening Section: The Make-or-Break Section

Listening is where dreams are made or broken. It's the last section, you're tired, and some tasks require perfect spelling under pressure.

Write From Dictation

This single task type can give you up to 18 points across multiple skills. Master this, and you're almost guaranteed to hit your target score.

‚ö†Ô∏è Write From Dictation Reality Check

You'll hear a sentence once. You must type it exactly, including spelling and capitalization. One wrong letter in "necessary" and you lose points. This is where SpellGuard becomes invaluable - it trains your muscle memory for perfect spelling under pressure.

Highlight Correct Summary

You'll hear a lecture and choose the best summary. It sounds simple, but the options are cleverly designed to confuse you.

üí° Summary Selection Strategy

Don't try to remember everything. Focus on the main idea and one or two key supporting points. The correct summary will capture the essence, not every detail.
